# Sources

Web references consulted during the creation of the mtg-worldbuilder skill.

## Worldbuilding Failure Stories & Retrospectives

- [State of Design 2018](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/state-design-2018-2018-08-20) — Ixalan tribal failures: four insular tribes with no cross-faction synergy, linear drafting
- [State of Design 2021](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/state-design-2021-08-16) — Kaldheim "too much in it" critique (ten realms, none deep); Strixhaven borrowed-resonance concerns
- [More Than Meets the Ikoria](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/more-meets-ikoria-2020-04-06) — Ikoria mutate design: creature-type restrictions loosened for playability, severing creative-mechanical link
- [Lessons Learned, Part 6](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/lessons-learned-part-6) — "One high-maintenance mechanic per set" lesson; Vision Design Summit creation
- [The Rabiah Scale, Part 3](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/the-rabiah-scale-part-3) — Five criteria for revisitable worlds: popularity, mechanical identity, creative identity, room for expansion, story continuation
- [Rabiah Scale — MTG Wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Rabiah_Scale) — Full plane revisitability ratings; Mercadia and original Kamigawa failure analysis

## Faction Design

- [Faction Packed (Rosewater, 2018)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/faction-packed-2018-10-15) — Four requirements for factions: mechanical identity, creative identity, relational definition, emotional resonance; graded faction sets (Ravnica A, Shadowmoor D-, Mirage F)
- [Schools in Session, Part 1](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/schools-in-session-part-1) — Strixhaven bottom-up design, enemy-color college structure
