## Canonical Rules and Mechanic-Difficulty References

These aren't sourced from a specific quote — they're the *background canon* that the catalog and templating files are calibrated against. Re-check against the live URL whenever rules templating shifts (every few months).

- [Magic: The Gathering Comprehensive Rules (official landing page)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/rules) — The authoritative rules document. Available in TXT, PDF, and DOCX. Filenames change with updates; always start from this landing page rather than bookmarking a specific PDF URL.
- [Hyperlinked Comprehensive Rules (Yawgatog)](https://yawgatog.com/resources/magic-rules/) — Searchable, hyperlinked HTML mirror of the Comprehensive Rules. Easier to navigate for spot-checking specific rules sections than the official PDF.
- [Storm Scale (MTG Wiki summary)](https://mtg.wiki/page/Storm_Scale) — Rosewater's 1–10 scale for the likelihood that a given mechanic returns to a Standard set. Useful for vision-time decisions about returning mechanics: a Storm Scale 8+ mechanic should not be your set's primary.
- [Storm Scale Ratings (community-aggregated index)](https://mtgstormscale.com/) — Searchable index of every Storm Scale rating Rosewater has issued; faster than searching Blogatog.
- [Storm Scale: Throne of Eldraine through Strixhaven, Part 1 (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/storm-scale-throne-of-eldraine-through-strixhaven-part-1) — Recent example of Rosewater's per-mechanic Storm Scale articles; useful template for assessing the return-likelihood of any 2019–2021 mechanic.
- [Storm Scale: Throne of Eldraine through Strixhaven, Part 2 (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/storm-scale-throne-of-eldraine-through-strixhaven-part-2) — Continuation; covers more mechanics from the same era.
- [Deciduous (Rosewater, 2022)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/deciduous-2022-03-28) — Defines deciduous as a category and lists the mechanics that qualify; companion to the evergreen list.
- [Reading the Designs (Rosewater, 2018)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/reading-designs-2018-03-05) — Already cited above for top-down/bottom-up; also the canonical reference for how to "read" a mechanic's complexity and place in the design hierarchy.
