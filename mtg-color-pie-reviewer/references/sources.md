# Sources

All web references consulted during the creation of the mtg-color-pie-reviewer skill.

## Canonical Color Pie Articles (Mark Rosewater)

- [Mechanical Color Pie 2021](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/mechanical-color-pie-2021) — Comprehensive P/S/T breakdown by mechanic; the primary source for `mechanical-color-pie.md`
- [Mechanical Color Pie 2021 Changes](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/mechanical-color-pie-2021-changes) — Detailed explanations of every change from the 2017 version, design philosophy behind each shift
- [Mechanical Color Pie 2017](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/mechanical-color-pie-2017-2017-06-05) — Previous comprehensive color pie breakdown; used for historical comparison
- [Let's Talk Color Pie](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/lets-talk-color-pie) — Foundational color pie principles, core values of each color, design relationships and evolution
- [Bleeding Cool](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/bleeding-cool-2015-04-06) — Color pie bending vs. breaking definitions, secondary color allocation philosophy

## Council of Colors

- [The Council of Colors, Revisited](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/the-council-of-colors-revisited) — How the Council evaluates cards, its structure, four-tier judgment framework (Fine / Second Look / Strongly Reconsider / Must Change)
- [The Council of Colors](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/council-colors-2016-08-22) — Original Council of Colors article establishing the evaluation system

## Color Philosophy & Identity

- [Ten Principles of Good Design, Part 1](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/ten-principles-good-design-part-1-2010-05-03) — Rosewater's core design philosophy underlying color pie reasoning
- [Ten Principles of Good Design, Part 2](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/ten-principles-good-design-part-2-2010-05-17) — Design framework continued

## Multicolor & Hybrid Design (Gold vs. Hybrid Rules)

- [As Good as Gold](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/good-gold-2018-09-04) — Gold/multicolor card design: synergy, parallelism, mirroring; Sphinx's Revelation and Recoil examples
- [Goldfingers](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/goldfingers-2009-04-17) — Multicolor design challenges, finding overlap for effects
- [The History of Hybrid, Part 1](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/the-history-of-hybrid-part-1) — Hybrid mana "or" philosophy vs. gold "and" philosophy
- [The History of Hybrid, Part 2](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/the-history-of-hybrid-part-2) — Hybrid design constraints, balance between two-color pairs

## Complexity & Rarity (NWO, Rarity Enforcement)

- [New World Order](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/new-world-order-2011-12-05) — Complexity management at common; informs rarity-based color pie enforcement
- [Common Knowledge](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/common-knowledge-2011-04-18) — Design philosophy for commons; why commons enforce pie strictest

## Keyword & Mechanic Classification

- [Ability Word to Your Mother](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/ability-word-your-mother-2008-07-28) — Keyword vs. ability word vs. named mechanic distinctions; relevant to identifying what effects are on a card
- [Deciduous](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/deciduous-2022-03-28) — Evergreen vs. deciduous keyword distinction; informs which keywords are "free" vs. set-specific
- [Keyword Play](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/keyword-play-2007-06-18) — Keyword design strategy across colors

## Rosewater Blogatog & Drive to Work

These informal sources provided specific color pie rulings, card-by-card verdicts (Beast Within, Hornet Sting, Chaos Warp, Harmonize), and evolved thinking on the pie:

- [Blogatog (Mark Rosewater's Tumblr)](https://markrosewater.tumblr.com/) — Ongoing Q&A where Rosewater rules on individual card color pie status; source for many verdicts in the wisdom catalog
- [Drive to Work Podcast Archive](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/drive-work-podcast-archive-2013-08-09-0) — Episodes on individual colors, the color pie, the Council of Colors, and specific card design stories
