## Wisdom Catalog Sources (Verified Failure Stories and Insights)

- [Storm Scale: Kaladesh and Amonkhet (Rosewater, 2019)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/storm-scale-kaladesh-and-amonkhet-2019-03-25) — Ian Duke on energy's "knife's edge" parasitic balance problem; energy rated Storm Scale 6
- [Head Designer Explains Why No Anti-Energy Cards Were Printed](http://epicstream.com/news/JakeVyper/Magic-The-Gathering-Head-Designer-Explains-Why-No-Anti-Energy-Cards-Were-Printed-in-Kaladesh) — Rosewater: "concerned that energy wasn't going to be played if it was too easily disrupted"; deliberate choice not to print counterplay
- [Perniciously Powerful Energy (Hipsters of the Coast, 2017)](https://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2017/11/perniciously-powerful-energy/) — Energy as a resource that "cannot be interacted with"; no safety valves
- [State of Design 2017 (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/state-design-2017-2017-08-28) — Energy's parasitism: "always got better if you played them with other energy cards"
- [Design 103 (Rosewater, 2006)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/design-103-2006-11-06) — Foundational modular/linear/parasitic framework; "You're building our decks for us" complaint
- [Rosewater Blogatog on Parasitic Mechanics](https://markrosewater.tumblr.com/post/643800218083672065/what-qualities-does-a-parasitic-a-mechanic-have) — Splice onto Arcane as canonical parasitic example; "if they made Arcane often enough, it wouldn't be parasitic anymore"
- [June 1, 2020 Banned and Restricted Announcement (Ian Duke)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/announcements/june-1-2020-banned-and-restricted-announcement) — Companion errata: 3-mana tax; "It's rare that we use a rules change to address metagame balance"
- [State of Design 2020 (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/state-design-2020-08-17) — Companions: "the biggest mistake of the set... the biggest mistake of the year"; Ikoria overtaxed Play Design
- [Ikoria Vision Design Handoff (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/ikoria-vision-design-handoff-2020-05-04) — Vision originally allowed multiple companions; monocolor → hybrid; what exploration produced vs. what survived
- [State of Design 2021 (Rosewater)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/state-design-2021-08-16) — Party: "didn't live up to its potential in Constructed"; succeeded in Limited but failed in Standard
- [2021 Mechanics in Review: AFR (Hipsters of the Coast)](https://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2021/12/2021-mechanics-in-review-adventures-in-the-forgotten-realms/) — Venture: "one of the most disappointing major mechanics ever"; ~1-1.5 mana value per room; dungeon choice was "actually a false one"
- [Are Dungeons a Parasitic Mechanic? (Hipsters of the Coast)](https://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2021/07/are-dungeons-a-parasitic-mechanic/) — Rosewater rates dungeons "medium parasitic"
- [Venture into the Dungeon Design (Wizards, 2021)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/card-preview/venture-dungeon-design-2021-06-30) — Three dungeons chosen as sweet spot; design challenges of external components
- [Resonate Spinning (Rosewater, 2019)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/resonate-spinning-2019-03-18) — Resonance defined as "building game components on information the audience already knows"
- [Twenty Years, Twenty Lessons Part 1 (Rosewater, 2016)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/twenty-years-twenty-lessons-part-1-2016-05-30) — All 20 design lessons including "don't confuse interesting with fun" and "restrictions breed creativity"
- [Twenty Years, Twenty Lessons Part 2](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/twenty-years-twenty-lessons-part-2-2016-06-06) — Lessons continued
- [Twenty Years, Twenty Lessons Part 3](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/twenty-years-twenty-lessons-part-3-2016-06-13) — Lessons concluded
- [Ten Things Every Game Needs (Rosewater, 2011)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/ten-things-every-game-needs-part-1-part-2-2011-12-19) — Interaction is requirement #3; directly relevant to counterplay evaluation
- [Everything About Parasitic Cards (Draftsim)](https://draftsim.com/mtg-parasitic-cards/) — Parasitism as a sliding scale; high density of independently playable cards at low end
