# Sources

Web references consulted during the creation of the mtg-art-director skill.

## Art Direction Process and Role

- [What Does a Magic Art Director Do?](http://sheppard-arts.blogspot.com/2017/05/what-does-magic-art-director-do.html) — Cynthia Sheppard's definitive blog post on the MTG art director role: volume of work (~578 pieces/year), the feedback process, what makes art direction succeed or fail
- [Interview with Cynthia Sheppard](http://casualhornan.blogspot.com/2013/11/interview-with-cynthia-sheppard.html) — Early career interview covering transition from freelance illustrator to WotC staff art director
- [Explore MTG Art: Cynthia Sheppard](https://scentofagamer.wordpress.com/2020/04/12/explore-mtg-art-cynthia-sheppard/) — Analysis of Sheppard's art direction philosophy and its influence on set visual identity
- [What It's Like Helping Shape the Artistic Worlds of Magic](https://gizmodo.com/what-its-like-helping-shape-the-artistic-worlds-of-magi-1844833007) — Interview covering the day-to-day workflow of MTG art direction, from concept to final approval
- [Cynthia Sheppard portfolio](http://www.cynthia-sheppard.com/about) — Background on Sheppard's role as Senior Art Director at WotC

## Art Description Format and Examples

- [You Make the Card 4 — Art Descriptions](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/making-magic/you-make-card-4-art-descriptions-2013-06-17) — Official WotC article publicly sharing the art description format used for card commissions
- [Ultimate Masters Art Descriptions](https://magic.wizards.com/en/news/feature/ultimate-masters-art-descriptions-2018-12-04) — Published art descriptions for Ultimate Masters cards, showing the Setting/Color/Action/Focus/Mood format in production use
- [MTG Illustration wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Illustration) — Comprehensive overview of MTG illustration history, process, and format conventions
- [List of Card Art Descriptions](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/List_of_card_art_descriptions) — Compiled list of publicly shared art descriptions from various official sources
- [WotC Freelance Art Submissions](https://company.wizards.com/en/freelance-art-submissions) — Official guidelines for artists working with WotC, including format expectations

## Art-Mechanics Mismatches and Failures

- [Magic Cards That Should Fly But Don't](https://commandersherald.com/magic-cards-that-should-fly-but-dont/) — Analysis of cards whose art depicts flight but lack the flying keyword, and vice versa
- [Magic Cards Where Artists Misunderstood the Brief](https://commandersherald.com/magic-cards-where-artists-misunderstood-the-brief/) — Examples of art-mechanics disconnects caused by unclear or misinterpreted art descriptions
- [Magic Cards with Confusing Art](https://commandersherald.com/magic-cards-with-confusing-art/) — Cards where the art actively misleads players about the card's function
- [Worst MTG Art](https://draftsim.com/worst-mtg-art/) — Analysis of art failures across Magic's history, including art-mechanics and color identity mismatches

- [In the Frame: Card Style Guides](https://outof.games/news/2483-in-the-frame-a-little-peek-into-magic-the-gatherings-card-style-guides/) — A look at actual MTG style guides and how artists use them

## Artist Perspectives

- [Interview with Sue Ann Harkey — Magic's First Art Director](http://blog.killgold.fish/2015/04/an-interview-with-sue-ann-harkey-magics.html) — The original MTG art director on establishing visual direction for the game
- [Interview with Melissa Benson](http://www.cardboardherald.com/articles/2016/12/17/interview-with-original-magic-the-gathering-artist-melissa-benson) — Original MTG artist on working with early art descriptions and creative freedom
- [My Time at Wizards](http://project-discovery.com/blog/2016/6/13/my-time-at-wizards-1) — Former WotC art director on the internal process, feedback cycles, and common art direction pitfalls
- [Jeremy Jarvis Interview — Art of MTG](https://www.artofmtg.com/interview-jeremy-jarvis-senior-art-director-wizards-of-the-coast/) — Jarvis on Innistrad's first-person camera technique, Bant's load-bearing architecture symbolism, Theros's contrapposto poses, and Sliver "moving-target syndrome"
- [Jeremy Jarvis Interview — CoolStuffInc](https://www.coolstuffinc.com/a/mikelinnemann-100114-an-interview-with-jeremy-jarvis) — In-depth on Jarvis's philosophy of art direction as visual storytelling
- [Why You Think All Magic Art Looks the Same](https://www.coolstuffinc.com/a/mikelinnemann-09062017-why-you-think-all-magic-art-looks-the-same) — Analysis of style guide conformity pressure on new artists vs. creative freedom for established ones
- [The Magic of Magic: An Artist's Perspective (Muddy Colors)](https://www.muddycolors.com/2020/01/the-magic-of-magic-the-gathering-an-artists-perspective/) — Artists' perspective on working within WotC's art direction system
- [Anatomy of an Illustration Prompt — Winona Nelson (Muddy Colors)](https://www.muddycolors.com/2023/05/anatomy-of-an-illustration-prompt-mtg-style/) — Nelson's breakdown of the MTG prompt structure: Setting, Color, Location, Action, Focus, Mood — described as "the most effective illustration prompt approach, fine-tuned for a very short pipeline"
- [Jeremy Jarvis wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Jeremy_Jarvis) — Background on the art director who defined modern MTG's visual standards
- [Jesper Myrfors — MTG's Original Art Director](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Jesper_Myrfors) — History of MTG art direction from the very beginning
